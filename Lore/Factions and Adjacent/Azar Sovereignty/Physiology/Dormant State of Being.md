# Dormant State of Being

Dormancy in the Azar is considered a utility or a punishment over all. Dormancy is achieved by mapping an individuals Neutronic Membrane into a temporally stabilized quantum lattice, often taking the form of small, pale green cubes. Once mapped, the Azar was said to be Dormant, being unable to process, receive, or understand stimuli, externally or internally. For all intents and purposes, the Azar stops being, frozen in a point of time. At a designated time, chosen by Azar court, the individual themselves, or the individuals family, they can be revived and receive a new frame to begin their life again.

## Purpose and History

Dormancy was originally designed during some of the earliest years of the Azars' existence as a stop-gap medical measure. As the Azar continued to refine their Neutronic Membrane technology and generation, older individuals began to be effected by a synthetic illness known as "Neutron Leakage Syndrome". This illness stemmed from flaws in the construction of the Neutronic Membrane worsened by mental or physical trauma. Veterans of the Scouring, born in the first and second generations were particularly susceptible to the illness.

Thus, Dormancy was designed as a way to safely, reliably store individuals without allowing their mind to dissolve further. Nearly 300,000 individuals were stored after the Scouring due to this illness, leaving less than 10,000 individuals active during this period. Thanks to the efforts of those that remained awake, the diseases was solved, and the individuals that laid Dormant were given new frames, with upgraded Neutronic Membranes that prevented further breakdown.

In modern time, the uses of Dormancy have grown varied, but three primary categories were formed.

### Law & Order

As the Sovereignty grew, a system of law and order was established to ensure the safety of its populace. Even with the Azar's culture, built around unity and an even playing field, as well as more perfected Neutronic mingling to create new individuals, some individuals will simply want to disturb the peace. The justice system forged by the Azar was swift, accurate, and exacting. Dormancy as a punishment was introduced after the Scarring, a political schism that divided the Azar, and resulted in a short lived civil war.

Members of the opposing party that were captured, and whom acted with extreme malice for their fellow Azar, were rendered permanently Dormant. Only criminals of the highest caliber are sentenced to Eternal Dormancy, a punishment that is viewed as worse than death by most individuals. For lesser crimes, Dormancy might be included with a prison sentence, alongside rehabilitation and other social services.

### Preservation

Preservation was the original purpose of dormancy, however in the modern Sovereignty, it has gained more than just medicinal use. With that in mind, the medicinal use has expanded, allowing mind-walkers to help perform memory recovery, therapeutic injections, and in the most extreme of cases, memory erasure. Preserved individuals may also be chosen by council, and given specific duties to preform when awoken, with specific instructions that must be kept off of the general network.

Finally, preserved individuals might also act as lorekeepers. People of great age, who have decided to be preserved for the sake of telling their story to those that wish to listen. While oral history is not the main method of record keeping within the Azar, it is still a respected tradition, maintained by many of the firstborn.

### Personal Choice

While rare, an individual can request to be put into a dormant state for personal reasons. In general these reasons seem to stem from an Azar version of ennui, driven by a lack of progress. Despite being functionally non-existent, some individuals claim that being moved to dormancy, then restored, gave them visions of what to do with their lives. This phenomenon, known as "Wake Up" Syndrome, is still undergoing research as to what actually seems to cause it.
