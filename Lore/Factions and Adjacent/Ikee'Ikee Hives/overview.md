# Ikee'Ikee Hives

Ikee'Ikee are a wandering, purely nomadic race that came to mild prominence during the Azar's creation era. They have no FTL, and instead, migrate using custom made "comets" that they lay eggs within, with a queen larva hibernating in its core. Their lifecycle is quite literally measured in the millions of years because of this.

## Articulation and Tool Use

Tool use is based on silk fabrications that link to an individual(s) limbs. Articulation resides primarily in modified antenna parts that have evolved opposable grips. The tarsus and claws at the end of each primary limb, as well as the elytron, and in females their horns. Males and females alike can both fabricate silk.

## Reproduction

Once a hive is established, the queen will copulate with a chosen male, resulting in the laying of 100-500 eggs at a time. These eggs will generally result in a 30-70 split female-to-male ratio. As the hives grow, females will accept males to copulate with, storing genetic material and picking and choosing when to fertilize their eggs, and with which partners material given the needs and capabilities of the hive.

Eggs develop for ~30 days, before hatching. Ikee'Ikee go through 5 instar stages each one lasting approximately 1 Terran year depending on the day length of the world they are on. After their 5th instar stage as a larva, they will pupate to reach adulthood. This will take another Terran year. Once an adult, an Ikee'Ikee individual can live for up to 5,000 Terran years. Once every 15-20 Terran years, an individual may undergo another pupation. These latter pupations usually only last 3-4 Terran months, resulting in the individual growing by ~10%.

## Feeding and Diet

Ikee'Ikee are omnivorous, with an extremely adaptable gut. Due to their very nature as nomads, a new hive must adapt to the world they land upon. While larval, Ikee'Ikee will seek out plant and fungal matter to consume. These initial years are incredibly important to promote the growth of a correct gut biome within the individuals. Once an Ikee'Ikee becomes an adult, they will generally rely on a mixture of flora and fauna for their diet.

Once a hive has lived its first generation, it will generally begin to form agricultural bays within their silken structures. Ikee'Ikee have been observed performing high intricacy farming as soon as their second generation, though it is generally limited to plants and animals local to their impact site. After this point, the hive will start selective evolution programs to breed plants and animals to be more useful to the hive in general.

Ikee'Ikee will also cook both animal and plant proteins for consumption in large communal pits. This cooking has been revealed to be a key milestone in hive evolution in a similar way to other Sophont species, resulting in the rapid acceleration of growth and advancement. It has also been noted that the Ikee'Ikee can taste, and will season their food, with individuals having preferences to the level of doneness and seasoning selections.

The most interesting items consumed by the Ikee'Ikee, are stones and metals. Ikee'Ikee will seek out metal ores, refine them, and consume the refined material. The hive might also use selective breeding to breed animals that provide these heavy minerals in higher quantities. These minerals are key to the production of silk of different qualities and uses.

## Communication

### Pheromones

Larval Ikee'Ikee cannot communicate via vocalization or writing, and primarily rely on pheromones for communication. During this developmental stage, larval individuals use several pheromones to signal needs, warnings, or anxieties. In general, at this stage of the lifecycle, an individual is not capable of full sapient thought. Adults can also produce pheromones, but they are usually only used for immediate emergency communication, or trail marking when vocalization or writing may not be available. Adults and larvae alike can sense these pheromones using specialized glands on their body.

### Vocal

Adult individuals gain the ability to vocalize. Ikee'Ikee vocalization is broken into 5 unique sounds; rasp, click, low thrum, high thrum, chatter. Through these five sounds, the entirety of their vocal language is forged. Rasp, Click, Low Thrum, and High Thrum are generated in their on individual resonating chamber within the individuals head. Chatter is achieved through the combination of Rasp and Click.

### Written

Ikee'Ikee have several writing systems, depending on the purpose of the written piece. While individuals generally have the visual acuity to read as other species do, they prefer to read via tactile feedback from their antennae. Writtings are written in a psuedo-generic alphabet, built around 83 meaning-core symbols, with 120 generic letters. Generic writing is performed usually by weaving patterns of silk with different densities into a tapestry. More important writings are usually negatively etched into stone, using silk to mask off areas to be etched around. These can be stored in tablets or done to stone monuments.

The most important writings, take the form extremely durable crystalline structures. These structures are often made of vitrified silk or metal, and are read uniquely among all other Ikee'Ikee writings. The reader is expected to use one of their opposable grips to tap a specific side of the crystal, where the other will pick up the reverberation. The shift in reverberation is used to denote the word or letter. This allows for extremely compact and robust data storage in an analogue way.

## Passing of Information

Ikee'Ikee pass information through both language, and genetic traits. As larvae, individuals have a very limited capacity for learning anything beyond survival tenants. Once an individual becomes an adult, they are far more capable of learning. The ability to read and write, seems to be inherently genetic to the species, allowing the first generation of a hive to begin to read and understand their history from the preserved materials within their comet-ship.

Once the hive is more formed, elder individuals are tasked with learning, and simplifying their species history for young adults to learn and apply. This includes anything from past cultures to farming techniques, to industrialization. Mid-adults are generally in a position to learn, and innovate on old ways to ensure the prosperity of the hive, and the ability to migrate again. Learning sessions are usually performed in groups, based on profession, in a similar way to classes programs are held in other species.

History is often spoken, rather than written, to younger individuals, with only that generations elders reading old and writing new. It's extremely rare for anything more than 3 migrations to be spoken of aloud, though Elders claim to memorize as much as they can. Oral storytelling is often used as a way to warn of the dangers of their temporary home, and induce a yearning to travel the stars once more, to start on the next migration.
