# ISCD Countermeasures

- **Gravity Cannon** – A high-powered gravity pulse that destabilizes an active ISCD compression corridor, forcing emergency corridor collapse and return to ordinary realspace traversal.
    
- **Metric Disruptors** – Devices that generate localized spacetime distortions, preventing stable internal corridor compression and degrading realized compression factor.
    
- **Compression Inhibitors** – Gravitational anchor points that prevent stable ISCD corridor formation, commonly deployed around strategic locations.
    
- **Navigation / Timing Scramblers** – Disrupt navigation and timing references, causing route miscalculations, phase timing faults, or emergency deceleration.
    

## Counter-Countermeasures

- **Gravitic Well Shaping** – Using precisely positioned gravitational wells to counteract or dissipate external gravitational disturbances.
    
- **Closed-Circuit Compression** – Dual-layered compression fields using additional planar rings to create a secondary stabilization envelope around the internal corridor.
    
- **Signal Jamming** – Remote hacking and jamming tactics to disable or bypass interference mechanisms.
    

## ISCD Failures & Hazards

- **Gravity Wells** – If a ship engages or exits too close to a gravity well without sufficient thrust margin, extreme acceleration and structural loading can cause hull fractures and ringframe stress.
    
- **Signal Disruptors** – If caught in an EM radiation field or signal jammer, a ship may drop out of ISCD operation while maintaining ordinary realspace velocity.
    
- **Faulty Navigation Data** – Incorrect ISCD calculations can route a ship into an asteroid field, a star, planetary atmosphere, or an unintended realspace emergence volume with damaged navigation systems.
